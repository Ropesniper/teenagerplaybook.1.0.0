Privacy Policy

How your information is collected, stored, and used

1. Data We Collect

When you register, we collect:

  • Email address — for account identification only
  • Username — your unique platform identifier
  • Password — stored as a secure bcrypt hash, never in plain text
  • Exercise history — session type, accuracy scores, pass/fail, and timestamps linked to your account

2. Data We Do NOT Collect

  • Camera footage or images — all camera processing is local (client-side)
  • Audio recordings — all microphone processing is local (client-side)
  • Browsing history or third-party tracking data
  • Location data
  • Any biometric templates or facial recognition data stored on servers

3. How Data is Stored

Account data and exercise history are stored in a MongoDB database hosted locally or on a designated private server. Only the platform administrator has database access. Your data is protected by JWT (JSON Web Token) authentication on all secure endpoints.

4. Session Data & Local Storage

Your session token may be stored in your browser's local storage (if you select "Remember me for 7 days") or session storage (for session-only access). This data is used exclusively to keep you authenticated and is cleared on expiry or logout.

Exercise history is also cached locally in your browser for offline access and merged with server records when available.

5. Data Sharing

Your personal data is never sold, shared, or disclosed to third parties. Data is used solely for platform functionality and the IB MYP project evaluation process.

6. Data Retention

Your account and history data is retained for the duration of the project. You may request deletion of your account and associated data by contacting the administrator.

7. Your Rights

  • Right to access your exercise history (available in the History section)
  • Right to delete your local history (available in History page)
  • Right to request full account deletion from the administrator

8. Security

Passwords are hashed using bcryptjs before storage. All authenticated API requests require a valid JWT token. The admin panel requires elevated permissions and a separate admin credential.
